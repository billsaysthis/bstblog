After the whole executive retirement storm, there are some positive stories coming out this week and with any luck the stock will remain above $7:

  • Sun retakes Unix sales lost to IBM - moving up to 54% market share of UNIX-based servers, all the 3.3% gain at IBM's expense.

  • Blueprints for Sun's New Direction - very positive article on the new Blueprints programs at Sun which give customers and tire kickers detailed instructions on installing and configuring complete solutions featuring Sun products at their core.

  • Microsoft ploy to block Sun exposed - one has to wonder how hard the execs at MS laugh at the rest of us sometimes.

  • Solaris 9 to Ease Patch Uploads - working to match some of the innovative Linux systems.

Somehow, I wonder if I'm speaking too soon, if Sun will ever get back to say $10 and save my butt.
